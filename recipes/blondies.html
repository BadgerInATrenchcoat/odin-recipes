Applebee's Copycat Blondies

Description

These Applebee's Copycat Blondies are a decadent dessert that replicates the popular menu item from the restaurant chain. The blondies have a soft, buttery texture and are topped with a rich maple cream sauce made with cream cheese, maple syrup, and brown sugar. The blondies are often sprinkled with chopped walnuts or pecans for added crunch and flavor. This copycat recipe allows you to enjoy the signature Applebee's blondie experience in the comfort of your own home.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/8 tsp baking soda
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1 c all purpose flour
  • 1/4 c chopped walnuts, optional
  • 1 c brown sugar, firmly packed
  • 5 Tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 Tbsp good vanilla extract
  • 1/2 c white chocolate chips (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Stir together baking powder, soda, salt, and flour. Sift dry ingredients into a medium bowl, stir in 1/4 cup nuts and set aside.
  3. In another, larger, bowl mix 1 cup brown sugar and 5 tablespoons melted butter. Combine well. Add eggs and vanilla and mix well, again.
  4. Stir flour mixture into butter/brown sugar mixture, a little at a time. When well mixed stir in white chocolate chips.
  5. Spread batter evenly in a non-stick 9 inch square pan. This will be a small amount of dough. The pan will not be as full as it would be for typical brownies.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until blondies pass the toothpick test. Remove from oven.
